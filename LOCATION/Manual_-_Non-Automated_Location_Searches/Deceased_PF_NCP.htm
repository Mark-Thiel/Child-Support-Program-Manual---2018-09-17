Deceased PF/NCP

Source:  Onandaga

If a customer demonstrates that the PF/NCP is deceased, TA may not refer that customer to CSS.  If the customer is referred, an LDSS-4882 must be completed even if the PF/NCP is deceased.

If  the PF/NCP dies on an open case, the worker must update the case remarks.

If a customer has been referred from the TA Unit and the PF/NCP dies, the worker must notify the TA Unit of the death via an  LDSS-2859 form.

See also:   "Death of a Child or a Party to an Order of Support: Questions and Answers," at:

Automated reporting of confirmed deaths

The Federal Case Registry (FCR) and the Social Security Administration (SSA) report deaths of CPs, NCPs, and children.

Searches of these two resources are included in automated PLS searches.

Automated notifications of deaths from these two sources are reported on the Worker Alerts > Enforcement Mode > FCR tab.

Deaths are recorded on the Case Mode > Status tab. (Specifically, the Q Group will be updated on that screen with code Q400.)

If the customer claims that the PF/NCP is deceased

If a TA referral customer claims to the CSEU that the PF/NCP is deceased, the customer must prove this in order to comply with cooperation requirements.  

When to launch manual investigations of possible deaths

The worker should manually investigate whether or not a PF/NCP is deceased if:

    • The customer has indicated this possibility during an interview.

OR

    • Extensive locate efforts using other means have not been successful.

 

Methods of manual investigations of deaths

    1. The worker must try to obtain the date of death from WMS or from the customer. The customer may have a copy of a death certificate, a newspaper (obituary) clipping, or they may know the date of death for certain. Any of these is sufficient for verification. If the client merely "heard that" the Respondent has died, or is unsure of the date, ask him/her for the source of the information, consider the source, and try to verify the death by other means.

    2. Search WRTS for the PF/NCP. If he or she died while active in some type of case (TA, MA, FS, etc.) the record may indicate that he or she is deceased.

    3. The CSEU worker can search archives of obituaries from newspapers across the U.S.A. and Canada at:  Obituaries.com.
