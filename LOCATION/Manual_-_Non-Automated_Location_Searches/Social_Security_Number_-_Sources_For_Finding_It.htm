Social Security Number:  Sources For Finding It

Source: 1989 Manual

The Social Security number of a PF/NCP is critical in the effort to locate and confirm the identity of that person and his/her resources.

To help find that information, the customer can check these resources:

 

Where You May Be Able to Find the SSN for a PF/NCP:

  1.  

 Payroll stubs; Forms W-2 or 1099

  1.  

 Statement of Benefits for Workers' Compensation

  1.  

 Tax Returns; particularly any joint returns filed during the past seven years (in cases involving joint returns, PF/NCP's SSN can be traced using the recipient's SSN)

  1.  

 Any of his/her correspondence with the Tax Department

  1.  

 Military documents (Note: The military currently uses the Social Security number as the serial number)

  1.  

 Life insurance policy

  1.  

 Credit applications

  1.  

 Credit agreement for purchases (such as furniture, refrigerator, television)

  1.  

 Old ID cards (health insurance, school ID, alien registration card)

  1.  

 Marriage license

  1.  

 Any personal documents from the Social Security Administration

  1.  

 Documents from the Veterans Administration

  1.  

 College or school records

  1.  

 Medical or dental records or bills

  1.  

 Public assistance/WMS records

  1.  

 Birth Certificate
