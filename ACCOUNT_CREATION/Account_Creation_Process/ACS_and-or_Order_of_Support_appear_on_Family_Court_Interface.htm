ACS and/or Order of Support appear on Family Court Interface

Check the The Modules > Court > Reports tab every day.  The appearance of an Order of Support or Account Creation Summary (ACS) on the Court > Reports tab means that the Family Court has issued an Order of Support, and the CSEU must build an account for it.

What is an Account Creation Summary (ACS)?

As its name implies, the Account Creation Summary (ACS) is meant to be a summary of all the information needed to create an account for a child support order.

When court staff enter information about a new or modified order into the Universal Court Management System (UCMS), an Account Creation Summary (ACS) is created.

Generally, the ACS is available via the Modules > [Family] Court [Interface] > Reports tab within 30 minutes of court staff entering the information into the UCMS.  In theory, the CSEU should be able to create an account and ledgers using the ACS -- without waiting for the actual Order of Support.

Often, the ACS appears on the Family Court Interface before the actual Order of Support does. The Order of Support may appear on the Family Court Interface several days after the ACS does.

Some districts see discrepancies between information provided in the ACS and information provided in the Order of Support. For this reason, some districts prefer to wait for the actual Order of Support, rather than building an account and ledgers from an ACS.
