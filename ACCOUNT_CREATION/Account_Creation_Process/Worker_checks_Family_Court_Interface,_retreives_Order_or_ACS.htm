Worker checks Family Court Interface, retrieves Order of Support or Account Creation Summary (ACS)

Check the The Modules > Court > Reports tab every day.  The appearance of an Order of Support or Account Creation Summary (ACS) on the Court > Reports tab means that the Family Court has issued an Order of Support, and the CSEU must build an account for it.

To retrieve an ACS from the Family Court Interface:

    1. Select Modules.

    2. Select Court from the drop-down menu.

    3. Select Messages from the drop-down menu.

The Court screen, Reports tab appears.

    1. Enter Docket Number or Case ID if available; otherwise select the ACS from the Document field and the appropriate District in the District field.

    2. Click the Search button.

What Information Does the ACS Provide?

 

How to check the Family Court Interface and retrieve an ACS

  1. Go to: Modules > Court > Reports tab

     

The Court > Reports tab appears.

Each date listed on the screen has two line items:

        • Action [Report]

          The Action Report lists all child-support-related court actions taken in family court the previous day.

          For example, in the screenshot below, you can see in the first line that on Report Created Date 05/08/20XX, there are 26 court actions. They are all contained within a single Action Report.

        • ACS [Report] 

          The ACS Report lists all child-support-related Account Creation Summaries created the previous day.

          For example, in the screenshot below, you can see in the second line that on Report Created Date 05/08/20XX, there were 30 ACSs created. All 30 ACSs are contained in a single ACS Report.  They are all contained within a single Action Report.

          If there were 30 cases yesterday that need accounts created for them, that means there were 30 support orders issued in court yesterday. They could be temporary or permanent orders.

New Action Reports and ACS Reports appear on the Modules > Court > Reports tab five days a week.
