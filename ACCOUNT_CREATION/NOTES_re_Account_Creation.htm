NOTES re Account Creation

  1. In order to issue an IWO, the Wage Order Delinquency field in ASSETS must contain a value of at least $1.00 (one dollar).  

The requirement to have a value of at least $1.00 (one dollar) in the Wage Order Delinquency field in the case record is purely a technological requirement of ASSETS.  

The reason for this requirement?

In the past, IWOs were issued only in cases where NCPs with support orders were neglecting to make their legally-required child support payments (i.e. these were delinquent accounts).  In such cases, an Income Withholding Order (IWO) was issued for the purpose of deducting child support payments directly from an NCP's paycheck â€” as an enforcement measure.

These days, an Income Withholding Order is no longer an enforcement measure, per se, used only on delinquent accounts. Instead, an IWO is the standard and preferred collection mechanism for all accounts -- not just delinquent accounts.

Nonetheless, for purely technological cause-and-effect reasons, ASSETS needs a "delinquency" on an account to justify the issuing of anIWO. Therefore, to satisfy this technological requirement, workers typically enter a value of $1.00 (one dollar) into the Wage Order Delinquency field so that ASSETS will allow an IWO to be generated for the case.
