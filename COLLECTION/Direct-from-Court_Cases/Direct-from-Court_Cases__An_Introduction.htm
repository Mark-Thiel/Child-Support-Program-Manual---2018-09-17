Direct-from-Court Cases:  An Introduction

CSEU must have a signed application or petition requesting services on file

Courts sometimes issue orders for child support payable to the SCU as part of related court proceedings and forward those orders to the CSEU. (Note: In direct-from-court cases, both location and paternity have already been established.)

However, such an court order alone does not qualify as an application for child support enforcement services under the federal and state requirements.

The order must be accompanied by a petition, application, or motion that includes a statement signed by the individual requesting services clearly indicating that such person is applying for child support enforcement services. (See DRL §§ 236B(7)(b) and 240(1)(a); FCA §§ 423 and 523; and SSL § 111-g).

The exception is where the individual has already applied for child support services in another SSD or state.

If there's no signed request for services from the applicant:

If the CSEU receives a court order for child support payable to the SCU without a signed statement by the individual requesting services, the CSEU must mail an LDSS-4882 to the applicant within two days.

The applicant must then complete and sign the LDSS-4882 (including checking Box 1 in Section C, and return it to the CSEU. This can be done by mail.

There is no interview required for direct-from-court cases

Applicants in direct-from-court cases are not required to come in for a face-to-face interview after they mail in the application. (Note: In these cases, when the applicant mails in the signed LDSS-4882, it usually contains enough information for the worker to build the case and initiate location and paternity establishment activities. If it does not, the worker may phone the applicant for further information, or may ask the applicant to come in for an interview to obtain further information.)

Minimum information required to build a case direct-from-court

  1. Where a non-TA customer has applied for child support services through the court, the SCU must require all such petitioners of such cases, except public officials in their own capacities, to provide the following data. In cases from Family Court, some of this information is available through the Family Court Interface and/or the Account Summary Creation, which can both be accessed through the ASSETS interface.

    1. client's name

    2. client's address

    3. name(s) and date(s) of birth of all children under the subject order

    4. client's Social Security Number

    5. a statement by the client of whether she/he is in receipt of, or has applied for Home Relief or Aid to Dependent Children

    6. such other information as the Department may require.

CSEU worker should retrieve ACS (Account Creation Summary) (Family Court only)

In direct-from-court cases that come from Family Court, the worker should retrieve the ACS (Account Creation Summary) via UCMS (the Universal Case Management System). The ACS contains names, addresses, SSNs, and employment statuses of the parties; names, DOBs, and SSNs of the children; and name of the NCP's employer.

Direct-from-court cases are processed like Non-TA cases

Once the CSEU receives a court order and accompanying signed statement requesting services, or a completed and signed LDSS-4882, the case is built and processed as a regular application (i.e. it is handled the same as a Non-TA case).
