Introduction to Support Enforcement

Source: 03 ADM 5

On a daily basis, each account is reviewed automatically by ASSETS to determine if payments due were actually received.

When a payment past due, ASSETS automatically records the non-payment as a delinquency and maintains a delinquency balance. Appropriate enforcement action is then initiated by ASSETS to secure payment.  ??MT- Include link to Lillith's enforcement calculator?

Enforcement actions include the following:

 

    • Bank Account Seizure

    • Credit Reporting

    • Federal and State tax refund offset

    • Interception of Personal Injury Settlements/Workers Compensation Benefits

    • IWO

    • Lottery intercept

    • Passport Denial Program

    • Referral to the State Tax Department

    • Suspension of Driving Privileges

    • Unemployment Insurance Benefits intercept

    • U.S. Passport Denial

Federal Tax Refund Offset Program

This program/process collects past-due child support amounts from NCPs through interception of their federal income tax refunds.
