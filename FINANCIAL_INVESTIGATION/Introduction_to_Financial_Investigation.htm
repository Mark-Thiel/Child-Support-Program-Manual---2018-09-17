Introduction to Financial Investigations

Source:  03 ADM 05

Financial investigations are necessary for:

    • Support establishment proceedings

    • Support order modifications

    • Support order enforcement

The CSEU may need counsel's help in providing information to the court in admissible form.

  1. Financial Investigation

    Determine financial expenses of raising this particular child (e.g. special medical or educational needs?)

    To determine a noncustodial parent's ability to pay child support, child support staff will investigate the noncustodial parent's income and financial assets through inquiries to employers and banks. They also conduct computer searches of the State Wage Reporting System (WRS), the New Hire Reporting System (New Hires), and state and federal income tax information.

    Two effective tools for obtaining financial information about the PF/NCP are:

      • New Hire Reporting (Financial information and information regarding the employer will auto-populate the case record.)

      • Wage and Health Benefits Report  (This can be presented to the court with the Support Petition.)

    Presenting complete and accurate financial information is essential for the court to determine a right-sized child support order. The income and resources, including medical insurance, available to each parent will be taken into account when determining the financial obligation of the PF/NCP.

  2. Financial documents required for verification

    As indicated in the summons, financial documentation about each parent is requested by the court. It is the parents' responsibility to return this information to the court before the hearing or the day of the hearing, whichever the court requests. The documentation includes:

      • A Financial Disclosure Affidavit (included with summons)

      • A representative pay stub of each parent's income

      • A copy of their most recently filed tax returns

      • Proof of any other child support court orders

  3. Obtain court histories of the parties

    Obtain court histories of the parties from the Family Court Interface on ASSETS. Information available through the Family Court includes the following:

      • Orders already established for the parties

      • Whether or not a previous Support Petition has been filed and/or dismissed by the court

In order to determine the extent of an individual's ability to pay support, and to arrive at a right-sized order, the CSEU initiates an investigation into the PF/NCP's income and other financial resources and assets.  

Even if a PF/NCP fails to appear for a scheduled child support establishment hearing, and a default order is issued, the default order must be based upon the financial realities of the PF/NCP and his/her ability to pay -- and hence, upon a diligent financial investigation of the PF/NCP's financial situation by the CSEU.

This is important because setting an obligation amount so high that a PF/NCP cannot make payments serves no purpose, helps no one, and would result in the PF/NCP accruing arrears and facing escalating collection enforcement measures.

 

 

At a minimum, financial evidence submitted to court in pursuit of a right-sized order should include:

    1. WHBR (exists on paper, and is available via ECS)

    2. XXXX

    3. Any New Hire information/documentation (New Hires and accompanying information regarding employer and employee are documented in the ASSETS system itself. So how do we extract it to present it to the court?

On a typical case (where the PF/NCP has one or two jobs, and is not self-employed or in a cash business), a WHBR may suffice as financial evidence. However, prior the court hearing, the worker will look up everything they can in the Advanced Search mode, using the Family Unit Number (FUN, available via the Family Court Interface). Workers search in WRTS and check the CAN  and any DSS case numbers. They also look for any other court activity.

In cases where the PF/NCP is self-employed, works in a cash-only business, or seems to have a standard of living that seems at odds with the PF/NCP's reported income, a more in-depth financial investigation may be warranted.
