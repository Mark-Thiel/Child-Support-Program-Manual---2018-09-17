Discovery of Financial Evidence

 

  1. Direct Financial Evidence

      1. Bank and Checking Statements

      2. Union records

      3. Annuities, Pensions

      4. Affidavits of net worth

      5. Bankruptcy schedules

      6. Tax returns, pay stubs, W-2

      7. 1099s

      8. Business Records -- General

    •  Bank Statements

    •  Corporate check ledgers and credit card statements

    •  Corporate invoices

    •  D/B/As

    •  Installment contracts (UCC-1)

    •  Retirement plans, Annuities

    •  Payroll records

    • Financial Statements (assets, retained earnings, loans to shareholders, stock)

    9. Business Records – Cash Business

    •  Bank deposits

    •  Purchases, inventory

    •  Location, expenses

    •  Indirect Proofs — “Pizza boxes,” mechanics liens, extrapolation from observed sales.

    10.  Business Records -- Real Estate Rental

    •  Management contracts (is it a related entity?)

    •  Real property taxes

    •  Tenant roles and rents

    •  Separate tax return?

    11. Loan/mortgage applications

    •  May be found through county property, mortgage, and judgment records, data mining companies, bankruptcy schedules, credit reports, UCC-1 filings, DMV records

    •  Loan payment amounts are indirect evidence (lifestyle)

    •  Income reported on loan application is direct evidence (party admission)

  2. Indirect Financial Evidence  (Imputing Income)

      1. Lifestyle – expense statements, luxury vehicles or other items, vacations, etc. Information may be found in CP interview, checking and credit card statements

      2. Credit reports

      3. Loans/mortgages

      4. UCC-1 statements

      5. DMV records

      6. Prior employments or income

      7. Real property

      8. Cars, boats, etc.

      9. Inheritances

      10. Stocks, bonds
