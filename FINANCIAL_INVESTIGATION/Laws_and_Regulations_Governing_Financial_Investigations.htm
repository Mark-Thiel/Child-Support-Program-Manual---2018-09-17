Laws and Regulations Governing Financial Investigations

 

    1. Recording Case Information

      1. Federal Regulation:

      45 CFR §303.4(b)(4). The final rule requires that child support agencies maintain documentation in the case record of information and evidence regarding the noncustodial parent’s (NCP’s) specific circumstances. This includes information acquired during the financial investigation and considered in determining the amount of imputed income.

      2.  State Regulations:

      347.8(a)(1)(iii) Maintain in the automated case record any information from new or modified orders of support, including the factual basis for the support obligation.

      347.8(a)(2)(v). Create a record of the information or documents obtained as a result of the financial investigation.

      347.8(a)(4)  In any proceeding to establish, modify, or enforce an order of child support, the child support enforcement unit shall review the record for information obtained from the financial investigation conducted on the noncustodial parent which may assist the court in making a factual determination regarding the noncustodial parent’s ability to pay child support in accordance with the standards set forth in section 240 of the DRL and section 413 of the FCA and provide such information to the court.

      3.  Impact

      The amendments expand the information about new or modified orders of support to be maintained in the automated case record to include information about the factual basis for the support obligation. The amendments also provide for the creation of a record of the information or documents obtained as a result of the financial investigation conducted by the support collection unit or child support enforcement unit (collectively referred to hereafter as the “CSEU”).

      The CSEU may use a checklist of sources of income, assets, and specific circumstances of the NCP to document and record information searched and/or obtained as a part of the financial investigation. The checklist should be retained in the case records (e.g., the ASSETS Document Log). A sample checklist/guidance document, the Financial Disclosure Desk Aid, is available on the Location page of the Electronic Resource System (ERS). See 17-ADM-11.

      Information about the factual basis for the support obligation, must be documented in the remarks of the case records and/or on a document electronically saved on ASSETS (e.g., Findings of Fact). In addition, the CSEU must update the Child Support Standards Act (CSSA) Information record in ASSETS to include information about the new or modified order.

      The CSEU is also required to provide the court with any information from the case record which may assist the court in making a factual determination regarding the NCP’s ability to pay or comply with the order of support. Obtaining and presenting this information in admissible form may require the assistance of counsel.

    2. Financial Investigations

      1. Federal Regulation

      45 CFR § 303.4(b) Child support agencies are required to review all available sources of information and document the sources that were reviewed, even if unsuccessful in discovering evidence of a parent’s income or ability to pay. The obligation should be based on evidence of the NCP’s ability to pay to the extent there is sufficient information known or available. In the preamble of the final rule, OCSE expressed a concern that orders for low-income parents are not based on the facts of the case but may be based upon a standardized amount above the means of the parent to pay.

      45 CFR § 303.6(c)(4). Child support agencies must establish guidelines for the use of civil contempt in IV-D cases. These guidelines must require that the IV-D agency screen the case record for information regarding the NCP’s ability to pay or comply with the order. The child support agency must also provide the court with information regarding the NCP’s ability to pay, or comply with the order, and his or her ability to pay the purge amount or comply with the purge conditions.

      2.  State Regulations

      347.8(a)(2) Prior to any proceeding to establish, modify, or enforce an order of child support, the child support enforcement unit shall take reasonable steps to determine the noncustodial parent’s income, financial circumstances, and ability to pay support in accordance with the child support standards set forth in section 240 of the DRL and section 413 of the FCA, by conducting a financial investigation pursuant to the following guidelines:

            1. Review available local, State, and federal information sources pursuant to section 347.7(b) of this Part, including, as appropriate, electronic data from other governmental agencies, State and national directories of new hires, and State and other tax information.

            2. Obtain information by case conferencing, interviews with either or both parties, mandatory financial disclosure, or questionnaires, as appropriate.

            3. Obtain information from the noncustodial parent or third parties using subpoenas or requests pursuant to sections 111-p, 111-r, and 111-s of the Social Services Law or article 31 of the Civil Practice Law and Rules, as appropriate.

      347.8(a)(4) In any proceeding to establish, modify, or enforce an order of child support, the child support enforcement unit shall review the record for information obtained from the financial investigation conducted on the noncustodial parent which may assist the court in making a factual determination regarding the noncustodial parent’s ability to pay child support in accordance with the standards set forth in section 240 of the DRL and section 413 of the FCA and provide such information to the court.

      347.8(c)(3)(ii). The child support enforcement unit must review the case file and provide the court with information contained therein regarding the noncustodial parent’s ability to pay, or otherwise comply with the child support order, which may assist the court in making a factual determination regarding the noncustodial parent’s ability to pay a purge amount or comply with any conditions set in a hearing pursuant to sections 454 or 455 of the FCA.

      3.  Impact

      The CSEU must take reasonable steps to determine the NCP’s income, financial circumstances, and ability to pay support before commencing a proceeding to establish, modify, or enforce a support obligation. This does not prevent the CSEU from investigation or discovery activities after the proceeding has commenced, especially if the circumstances or facts (or lack of facts) of the case merit further action.

      The regulations require that the CSEU: (i) screen the case for information regarding the NCP’s ability to pay or otherwise comply with the order; (ii) utilize other investigation or discovery methods if appropriate; and iii) provide the court with information regarding the NCP’s ability to pay or comply with the order. Providing the information in admissible form may require the assistance of counsel.

      Determining what level of financial investigation is appropriate and reasonable depends on the facts of the case and the parties’ circumstances. For example, if the NCP has stable employment, lives within his or her income, and the CP does not indicate any other sources of income. the mandatory financial disclosure required by FCA 424-a may be sufficient. If the NCP is self-employed, has expenses exceeding reported income, or the CP indicates that the NCP has unreported income or assets, a more searching inquiry may be needed.

      In enforcement actions, the CSEU must review the case file and provide the court with any information from the case record which may assist the court in making a factual determination regarding the NCP’s: 1) ability to pay the support obligation or otherwise comply with the order of support; or 2) ability to pay a purge amount or comply with any conditions set in a hearing pursuant to sections 454 or 455 of the FCA.

    3. Calculation of Support Obligation in Absence of Information About Ability to Earn

      1. Federal Regulation:

      45 CFR § 302.56. The federal regulation does not prohibit states from imputing income. However, if imputation of income is permitted, the guidelines must take into consideration the specific circumstances of the noncustodial to the extent known, including factors like the ones listed in the amended state regulation.

      2. State Regulation

      347.8(a)(2)(iv.)  If there is insufficient evidence of the noncustodial parent’s income or financial circumstances to use as the measure of the noncustodial parent’s ability to pay, then any recommendation by the child support enforcement unit regarding the support obligation shall be based on available information about the specific circumstances of the noncustodial parent, including assets, residence, employment and earnings history, job skills, education, literacy, age, health, criminal record or other employment barriers, record of seeking work, job market conditions, the availability of employers willing to hire the noncustodial parent, prevailing earnings level, and other relevant background  factors in the case to the extent such information is available and relevant.

      3.  Impact

      If there is insufficient direct evidence of the NCP’s income or financial circumstances to use as the measure of the NCP’s ability to pay, the court may be asked to impute income. NYS law current requires that any order based on imputed income be supported by information in the court record.

      The federal Final Rule requires that any recommendation by the CSEU regarding imputation of a support obligation be based on available information about the specific circumstances of the NCP, to the extent such information is available and relevant.     4. Responsibility to Modify if Financial Investigations Indicates a Change in Circumstances

      1. State Regulation:

      347.8(c)(2)(ii) If a review of the terms of the order of child support and/or the results of the financial investigation demonstrate a change in the parties’ financial or factual circumstances such that modification of the support obligation may be appropriate, the child support enforcement unit must immediately petition or otherwise assist the recipient of services to petition the Family Court or the Supreme Court, if the Supreme Court has retained jurisdiction over support issues in the case, for modification of the order of support.

      2. Impact and explanation of other changes

      The amendment to 347.8(c)(2)(ii) requires the CSEU to petition or otherwise assist the recipient of services to modify the order of support if a change in the parties’ financial or factual circumstances indicate that modification of the support obligation may be appropriate A petition may be filed for either an upward or downward modification.

    5. Enforcement - Notice

      1. Federal Regulation:

      45 CFR § 303.6(c)(4)(iii). Child support agencies are required to provide clear notice to the NCP that the ability to pay constitutes the critical question in a contempt or violation proceeding.

      2. State Regulation:

      347.8(c)(3)(i) Where an order of support payable to the custodial parent is not being complied with, the child support enforcement unit must ensure that any petition to the Family Court or the Supreme Court, if the Supreme Court has retained jurisdiction over support issues in the case, alleges a violation of the order of support, includes a request for the adjudication of the delinquency amount and the award of a money judgment, and provides clear notice to the noncustodial parent that his or her inability to pay child support as ordered is a defense to a finding of willful violation or contempt.

      347.9(d) Additional enforcement action.

      ... Every petition to find the noncustodial parent in violation or motion to hold a noncustodial parent in contempt of an order to pay child support shall provide clear notice to the noncustodial parent that his or her inability to pay child support as ordered by the court is a defense to a finding of willful violation or her inability to pay child support as ordered by the court is a defense to a finding of willful violation or contempt.…

      3. Impact:

      Every petition to find the NCP in violation, or motion in supreme court to hold a NCP in contempt, of an order to pay child support provide clear notice that the ability to pay constitutes the critical question in violation or contempt proceedings against the NCP. The petitions available through the Document Generation Utility of ASSETS include enhanced warning language providing clear notice to the NCP that his or her ability to pay constitutes the critical question.
