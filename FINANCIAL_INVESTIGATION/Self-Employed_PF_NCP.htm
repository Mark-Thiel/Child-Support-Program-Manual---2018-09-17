Self-Employed PF/NCP

Source:  Monroe Site Visit notes

If a PF/NCP is self-employed, or employed in an industry where a significant portion of his/her income is in cash or may be "off the books", a more in-depth financial investigation of this PF/NCP may be warranted.

The CP or other family or friends may be able to provide information about the PF/NCP's financial resources or assets.

The worker should check to see whether the PF/NCP is receiving any public assistance.

Investigating possible additional sources of income

The CSEU may ask the Social Security Administration for a Benefit Letter.

They may search for DBAs (sole proprietorships). Records of registered businesses (and their owners) are maintained by the office of the Secretary of State in each state.

The CSEU may also use the following resources for a more in-depth financial investigation:

    • Equifax

    • Social Media (LinkedIn, Facebook)

    • Credit Bureaus
