Court issues a Temporary Order

A Temporary Order of Support must contain an adjournment date. The adjournment date is the date of the next scheduled hearing.

See:  Form 4-6 - Temporary Order of Support & Referral to Support Magistrate

See:  Form 4-6a - Referral to Support Magistrate & Temporary Order of Support

    1. Each party receives a copy of the Temporary Order of Support.

      See:  Form 4-6 - Temporary Order of Support & Referral to Support Magistrate

      See:  Form 4-6a - Referral to Support Magistrate & Temporary Order of Support

    2. The CSEU receives the Temporary Order of Support according to local protocols, which may include mail, email, fax, courier, or some other method.

      See:  Form 4-6 - Temporary Order of Support & Referral to Support Magistrate

      See:  Form 4-6a - Referral to Support Magistrate & Temporary Order of Support

    3. The CSEU can also obtain a copy of the order via the Family Court Interface > Messages tab.

Actions Required of the Worker

When the CSEU receives a Temporary Order of Support payable through the SCU, the worker must build accounts and ledgers for it, just as for a permanent Order of Support.

See:  Form 4-6 - Temporary Order of Support & Referral to Support Magistrate

See:  Form 4-6a - Referral to Support Magistrate & Temporary Order of Support

Procedures for building accounts and ledgers for temporary orders are the same as for permanent orders.
