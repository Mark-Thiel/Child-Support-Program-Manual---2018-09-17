Does either party request an adjournment?

Either party can request an adjournment. A hearing can be adjourned for any number of reasons. One such reason might be that one or both of the parties needs time to hire an attorney.

Legal services for TA cases

Foster care, TA, or Medicaid cases are provided legal services at no charge.  However, in these cases, the DSS is the Petitioner, and the attorney represents the DSS —  the customer appears only as a witness in the case.

Legal services for Non-TA cases

In Non-TA cases, the customer can:

      • Elect to forego the services of an attorney, and represent himself/herself in court

OR

      • Hire an attorney privately

OR

      • Apply for legal services through the CSEU  

If the Non-TA customer is the CP, and wishes to apply for legal services, he or she must indicate this by checking Box 1 in Section C – Application/Affirmation for Child Support Services on the LDSS-4882.

The CSEU is entitled to recover the costs for legal services provided to Non-TA customers. Therefore, the customer must complete and sign the "Right to Recovery Agreement for Legal Services" --  form LDSS-4920.
