About Me

I lift my lamp beside the golden door.

I taught English in the Japanese public school system for three years.

In NYC, I have personally taught thousands of people to speak English. The average age of my students was about 25 years old.

Many of the people I have taught are or were undocumented immigrants. They were some of the best people I've ever met.

Some of my students would work a 16-hour shift in a kitchen, get out of work around 7am, and come straight to my ESL class at 8am.

If the culture of your company or organization is anti-immigrant, anti-black, anti-mu

I seek to work with smart, creative people who are citizens of the world.

 

If you prize the American flag over the American Constitution, judge people on the color of their skin rather than the content of their character  -- or, in general, prize form over substance -- please look elsewhere for a technical writer.

This has included both documented and undocumented immigrants, as well as short-term visitors, visiting university students, etc.

 

I'm an American born and raised.  I'm from New York.

I speak Spanish fluently.  I used to speak Japanese well, but haven't spoken it since 1993.

A Word About My Values

 

Not like the brazen giant of Greek fame,

With conquering limbs astride from land to land;

Here at our sea-washed, sunset gates shall stand

A mighty woman with a torch, whose flame

Is the imprisoned lightning, and her name

Mother of Exiles. From her beacon-hand

Glows world-wide welcome; her mild eyes command

The air-bridged harbor that twin cities frame.

"Keep ancient lands, your storied pomp!" cries she

With silent lips. "Give me your tired, your poor,

Your huddled masses yearning to breathe free,

The wretched refuse of your teeming shore.

Send these, the homeless, tempest-tost to me,

I lift my lamp beside the golden door!"
