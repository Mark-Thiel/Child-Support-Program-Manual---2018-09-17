Conventions Used In This Manual

 

ADMs (Administrative Directive Memorandums)

In this manual, ADMs are referred to in the following syntax:

10-ADM-02

Notice the use of the hyphens, and that both numbers are represented as two-digit numbers.

(two-digit number) (hyphen) (ADM) (hyphen) (two-digit number)

In conducting research for this manual, the author has seen ADMs referred to in several different ways, including:

  • 10-ADM 02

  • 10 ADM-02

  • 10 ADM 02

  • 10-AMD-02

Notice the variations in the use of the hyphen.  

The last syntax option (10-ADM-02) is the best, because it is (technologically) the most searchable term.

8th and 9th characters

 

The first two characters are a two-digit number (10) that denotes the year the ADM was issued.  ("10" = 2010)

 

 

The third character is a hypen. The hypen is required.

 

 

The fourth, fifth, and sixth character are the acronym "ADM" (Administrative Directive Memorandum).

 

 

The 7th character is a hyphen.  The hypen is required.

   

The 8th and 9th characters denote the number of the ADM for the year it was issued..   

  

Example:

  

10-ADM-02

  

This ADM was written in the year 2010.  It is the second (02) ADM issued in 2010.
