  1. Major Functions of the Child Support Program

    Major functions include:

    • Intake

    • Location of absent parents

    • Paternity Establishment

    • Support Establishment

    • Support Collection

    • Support Enforcement

    • Case Closure

  1. Child Support is a successful and cost-effective government program

    For every dollar that it spends, the child support program collects $4.99.

    In 2017, $1.83 billion was collected on behalf of custodial parents and their children. Ninety-five percent of that money went to directly to families, and 5% reimbursed public assistance dollars.

    There were approximately 855,000 child support cases in the state of New York in 2017.  Ninety percent of children participating in the child support program in NYS have had their paternity established, and 87% of open cases have a child support order established.

    Income withholding is the standard mode of collection in New York State. In 2017, 73% of child support collections were withheld from an NCP's paycheck.

    In 2017, approximately 42% of children in NYS were born out of wedlock. Of those, in approximately 67% of cases, the parents signed an Acknowledgement of Paternity in the hospital at the time of the child's birth.
