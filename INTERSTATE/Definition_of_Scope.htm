Definition of Scope

In the context of the Child Support Program, "interstate" mean that the case in some way crosses the state lines. This could mean, for example, that one parent is in New York and one is in another state; both parents are in New York but their child support order was issued by another state; or neither parent is in New York but a New York child support enforcement unit is providing services on the case.

The term "state" refers to any state of the U.S., as well as the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, Guam, and the U.S. Virgin Islands.

The category "interstate" is distinct from "local" (involving only one state), "inter-county" (involving more than one county within a single state), "international" (involving more than one nation), and "tribal" (involving the jurisdiction of an Indian nation or tribe).

Many aspects of an interstate case are the same as for local cases (such as building a case and account in ASSETS, monitoring Worker Alerts for actions needed, and implementation of automated enforcement measures after an account is built). This chapter addresses only the specific aspects that are unique to interstate cases, and is oriented to the caseworker perspective.
