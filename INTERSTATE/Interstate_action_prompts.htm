Action Prompts and Corresponding Responsibilities

These are the prompts that indicate you need to do something regarding an interstate case, and the steps you need to take when they occur:

  • If you need to initiate the establishment, enforcement, or modification of an order, and you find that the case involves a party in another state or an order issued by another state, use the Interstate Case Assistant to identify how to proceed, then take the actions it directs you to do.

  • At least one person in each district needs to review the CSENETINFOREPORT every day to check for messages from other states. If you are responsible for doing that:

    1. Access the report daily through COLD.

    2. Use the CSENETINFOREPORT Action Guide to identify the response needed for the messages that have come in.

    3. Take the actions directed by the Guide.

  • If an action for establishment, enforcement, or modification of an order is already in progress for an interstate case, and you need to ask for information from or provide information to another state, looks on the "Case needs something besides establishment, enforcement, or modification" page of the Interstate Case Assistant to identify how to proceed, then take the actions it directs you to do.

  • Other than the initiation of an action, the working of the CSENETINFOREPORT, and the exchange of information, the processing of an interstate case at the district level of the child support program is essentially the same as in a local case. Therefore, each day, review and respond to Worker Alerts messages, COLD reports, and ECS referrals. By doing that as a standard part of caseload management, you will address the tasks needed for interstate cases as they arise.
