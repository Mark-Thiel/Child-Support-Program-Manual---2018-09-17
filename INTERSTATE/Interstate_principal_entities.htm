Principal entities

Interstate cases involve several key entities that do not arise in local case processing:

  • Initiating state is used to refer to the state of the child support agency that asks for action to be taken or information to be provided by an entity in another state. For example, in the statement "New York wants the order modified by California," New York is the initiating state, and "New York" is understood to mean "the agency within New York" rather than the state government as a whole.

  • Responding state is used to refer to the state of the entity that is asked to take action or provide information at the request of a child support agency in another state. For example, in the statement "New York wants the order modified by California," California is the responding state, and "California" is understood to mean "the child support agency and/or tribunal within California" rather than the state government as a whole.

  • Central registry is a state-level unit within each state's child support agency. Its function is to be the central point of the initial contact from another state in any case being processed through the child support program. For example, if New York wants California to modify an order, to initiate the request New York would send documents needed to California's Central Registry, which would review and then forward the documents to the appropriate local destination within California. Thereafter for that case, New York would communicate directly with the local office within California.

New York's Central Registry is part of the processing center and is responsible for processing incoming interstate requests.

  • Tribunal is the entity that a state has designated, in its UIFSA statute, as having the authority to conduct formal proceedings in interstate cases (such as establishing or modifying an order, or holding a hearing for enforcement). UIFSA specifies the rights, responsibilities, and limitations of the tribunal. In some states the tribunal is a court, in some it is the child support agency itself, and in some the responsibilities are distributed.

New York's tribunal is family court.
