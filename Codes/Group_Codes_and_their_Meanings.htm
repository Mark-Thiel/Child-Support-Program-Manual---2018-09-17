Group Codes and Their Meanings

Note:  "Group Code" refers to the letter of the alphabet that makes up the first position of a four-character Status Code.

 

Status Codes appear on the Case Mode > Status Information tab.

 

Helpful tip:  Looking for a specific Status code? From anywhere in this manual, just put the code into the Search box and hit Enter.  (Example: P208 + Enter, or L778 + Enter)

Set pop-up properties to 800 wide by 1000 high.

There are many different kinds of codes relevant to the ASSETS computer program. The Status and Reason Codes, though, are particularly important because they give (in great detail/granularity) ongoing status reports/updates for any given case. The Status Codes can give a worker a snapshot of the current status of a case, as well as a step-by-step, chronological, granular history of the case.

Virtually any and all actions taken on a case or affecting a case – whether they are fully automated actions, court actions, or actions performed by workers at the CSEU or the Processing Center — are reflected in and reported to the worker via Status and Reason Codes.
