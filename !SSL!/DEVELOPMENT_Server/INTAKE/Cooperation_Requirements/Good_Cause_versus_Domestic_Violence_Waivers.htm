Good Cause

Good Cause is determined by the TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). Unit. The following are legitimate reasons for Good Cause A legal reason for which a Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF) recipient is excused from cooperating with the child support enforcement process, such as past physical harm by the child’s noncustodial parent. It also includes situations where rape or incest resulted in the conception of the child and situations where the mother is considering placing the child for adoption.:

    1. The customer feels that cooperation with child support will result in serious physical and/or emotional harm to themselves or to the child.

    2. The child was conceived as a result of rape or incest.

    3. A legal adoption is pending.

    4. The caregiver is receiving pre-adoption counseling for up to three (3) months after the birth of the child.

Domestic Violence

Under the Family Violence Option in New York State, procedures are in place to screen TA applicants or recipients for domestic violence when applying for TA benefits.

This option can provide service referrals to the CP Custodial Parent or Custodial Party. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child., and/or temporary waivers from the requirement to cooperate with the Child Support Program. This is determined by a specially trained Domestic Violence Liaison, and must be honored by the Child Support Program.

Referral Procedure for Domestic Violence Screening

Sometimes, a customer may not make a disclosure of domestic violence when initially screened during a TA eligibility interview, but later discloses domestic violence during a child support interview, when compliance with the TA program requirements are discussed in more detail.

If this happens, the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit worker must stop the interview and refer the customer to the DVL, following local protocols.

Child support workers cannot conduct a domestic violence screening.

Local district procedures and protocols for referring clients to be screened must be followed. (Some districts may refer the customer back to the TA Unit, which will do the screening and make a referral A request sent to a CSEU from another jurisdiction or a non-IV-D agent or agency asking that a child support order be established. to the DVL. Other districts may refer the client directly to the DVL.)

Therefore, it is important to know and follow the procedures in your own particular local district.
