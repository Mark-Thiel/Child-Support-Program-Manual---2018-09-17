If a customer wants services but is afraid for his or her safety, the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit can work with the him or her in the following ways:

    1. Remove the customer's location and/or employment information from petitions, notices, or any required financial disclosure, subject to approval of the court.

(Note: While a customer's address can be suppressed from all forms, it is not always possible to suppress the name of the county CSEU or the court that issued the order.)

    1. Request that the court not disclose location or employment information about the customer

    2. Request that any court-ordered laboratory appointments (for genetic testing Analysis of inherited factors of the mother, child, and alleged father, which can help to prove or disprove that a particular man fathered a particular child., for example) be scheduled for separate dates for the CP Custodial Parent or Custodial Party. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. and the NCP.

    3. File paternity and support petitions on behalf of customer without requiring the customer to appear in court with the alleged abuser, or, if testimony Evidence that a competent witness gives under oath at trial or in an affidavit or deposition. of customer is required, request that the court make adequate provision for the customer's safety while in court

    4. Provide all enforcement services without involvement of the customer

After receiving this information, and a discussion of the safety concerns, the customer wishes to apply for child support services, he or she must complete and sign the LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882).
