Worker examines the supporting documentation

Source: 12-ADM-03

  1. Examine the supporting documentation:

      1. Compare information listed on the LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882) to information in the supporting documentation.  

For example, verify that each party’s name as listed on the LDSS-4882 reflects the exact first name, middle name or initial, last name, and any applicable suffix as recorded on the provided supporting documentation.  

      1. Verify the listed information regarding each party’s:

 Date of birth.

 Employer information.

 Mailing and residential addresses, including floor number and apartment or suite number.

 Social Security number (SSN Social Security Number.) or Individual Taxpayer Identification Number (ITIN Individual Taxpayer Identification Number. The ITIN is a tax processing number issued by the Internal Revenue Service (IRS) that must begin with the number 9. For more information on the ITIN, refer to the "Dear Colleague" letter dated April 12, 2005 regarding the entering of ITINs), or other identification.

 

      1. Reconcile any discrepancies by contacting the applicant to determine the correct information.

  1. Identify information to be referenced when building the case.

  2. Determine which documents have evidentiary value for court proceedings.  

The CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit should retain documents such as:

 Award letters

 Benefit notices or letters

 Educational and unreimbursed health care expenses 

 Most recently filed federal tax returns and all schedules

 Pay stubs

 Proof of child care

 W-2 forms
