Worker provides customer with legal rates

The worker provides the customer with form LDSS-4920 Form LDSS-4920 is the "Right to Recovery Agreement for Legal Services." This must be completed by Non-TA applicants who are requesting legal assistance services. The form is available in Spanish, as well, as Form LDSS-4920SP., the Right to Recovery Agreement for Legal Services.

The LDSS-4920 has a blank field where the legal rate should appear.

The CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit worker must:

    1. Fill in the rate for legal services.

    2. Explain the legal rates to the customer.

    3. Give the form to the customer.

Additionally, the worker must explain to the customer that:

    1. The attorney is the legal representative of the Commissioner of the Social Services District, and does not represent the customer personally.

    2. There is no attorney-client privilege between the customer and the attorney.

    3. Attorney services are limited to establishment of paternity and the establishment, modification A formal written application to a court requesting a change in an existing child support order. For a court to grant a modification in the amount of a child support order, the petitioner for the modification must prove a significant and unforeseen change in circumstances (e.g. laid off from job, child has developed an unforeseen medical condition, etc), adjustment, and enforcement of support obligations.

Form LDSS-4920 is available in Spanish, as well: LDSS-4920SP (Acuerdo Sobre Derechos de Recuperacion de Pagos por Servicios Legales).
