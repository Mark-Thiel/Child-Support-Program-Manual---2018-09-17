Referral to Domestic Violence Liaison

Source:  12-ADM-03

If a customer indicates a safety concern on the LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882), the worker must stop the intake interview and refer the customer — following local protocols  — to a Domestic Violence Liaison (DVL) An employee specially trained in assessing client claims of domestic violence, granting waivers from Temporary Assistance requirements, and referring clients to needed services. for screening and assessment.

In TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). cases, the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit must notify the TA Unit of this action via the LDSS-2859.

All CSEU activities must be suspended during the domestic violence screening and assessment process and until a waiver decision is made by the Domestic Violence Liaison.
