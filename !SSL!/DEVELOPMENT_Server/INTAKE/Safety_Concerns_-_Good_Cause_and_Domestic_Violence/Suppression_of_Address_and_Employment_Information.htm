Suppression of Address and Employment Information

Source:  12-ADM-03

The CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit must ensure that the customer's address and employment information do not appear on any documents, notices, summons A notice to a defendant or respondent that an action against him or her has been commenced in court and that a judgment will be issued against him or her if the complaint is not answered within a certain time., etc. that are created by the CSEU as part of providing a CSEU service. SSDs should follow local protocols with regard to listing a substitute address for the customer on ASSETS Automated State Support Enforcement and Tracking System (ASSETS)..

If the customer chooses to request address confidentiality from the Family Court Family Court hears cases involving adoption, child abuse and neglect, foster care approval and review, guardianship, PINS (Persons in Need of Supervision), juvenile delinquency, child support, custody, visitation, spousal support and family offenses (Orders of Protection). Divorce actions are heard in Supreme Court., the CSEU should generate the Address Confidentiality Affidavit, which is available on ASSETS as a template in the State Folder of the Local Correspondence tab in the Document Generation module.

Alternatively, the customer may complete Family Court General Form 21 (GF-21), Address Confidentiality Affidavit, which is available at www.nycourts.gov.

The Address Confidentiality Affidavit must be filed with the Family Court.

If the request for confidentiality is accepted, the Family Court will issue Family Court General Form 21a (GF-21a), an Address Confidentiality Order.   The order will contain information regarding the person designated as the agent for service of process The actual delivery of legal paperwork that requires a person to respond or appear to that person or his/her agent. and all papers in the case.
