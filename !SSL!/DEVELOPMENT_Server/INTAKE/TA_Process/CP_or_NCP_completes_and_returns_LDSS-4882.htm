CP or NCP fills out an LDSS-4882

Source:  12-ADM--03

All customers requesting child support services must complete and sign an LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882).

* The exception to this is direct-from-court cases in which a court order for child support is accompanied by a request for child support services in the form of a petition A petition is a court document reflecting a request made prior to a hearing. A petition may be attached to a court order., motion, or other court document signed by the customer/petitioner.

When There's More Than One NCP or PF — LDSS-4882B

The Child Support Application/Referral A request sent to a CSEU from another jurisdiction or a non-IV-D agent or agency asking that a child support order be established. form (LDSS-4882) has space for only one NCP Non-Custodial Parent. Sometimes called the "absent parent."/PF Putative Father. This is a person who is presumed to be the father of a child, though there may not yet be hard evidence to support that presumption..

If support for the child is sought from more than one NCP, or the customer provides several possible putative fathers for the child:

    • For each additional NCP/PF, the customer must complete one form LDSS-4882B, Information for an Additional Noncustodial Parent (NCP) / Putative Father (PF).

    • Alternatively:  Copies of a blank Part II – Noncustodial Parent (NCP) / Putative Father (PF) Information (page A-3 and page A-4 of the LDSS-4882) may be made, completed, and kept on file in lieu of the LDSS-4882B.

The LDSS-4882B is available on ERS Electronic Resource System (ERS). ERS is an intranet website maintained by NYS Child Support Services specifically for the use of local and state staff of NY’s child support program. The site provides access to online policy and program information to assist staff with their responsibilities. and on the New York State Child Support website at childsupport.ny.gov.  

If an LDSS-4882B or a completed copy of Part II – Noncustodial Parent (NCP) / Putative Father (PF) Information is attached to the LDSS-4882, the customer must check the box under Part II – Noncustodial Parent (NCP) / Putative Father (PF) Information at the top of page A-3 of the LDSS-4882 to indicate that information about additional NCP(s)/PF(s) is being attached to the LDSS-4882.

 

 

If the LDSS-4882B is used, the customer must indicate the number of LDSS-4882B forms attached to the LDSS-4882 by entering the page information at the top of page 1 of each LDSS-4882B.  

When There's More Than One Child — LDSS-4882C

The LDSS-4882 has space to accommodate information for only one child.  If the application is for a case involving more than one child, the applicant must provide information about each additional child.  

    • The customer must complete and sign an LDSS-4882C, Information for an Additional Child form for each additional child..  

    • Alternatively, a copy of a blank Part III - Child Information (page A-5 of the LDSS-4882) may be used.

The LDSS-4882C is available on ERS and on the New York State child support website at childsupport.ny.gov.  

If an LDSS-4882C or a completed copy of Part III – Child Information is attached to the LDSS-4882, the applicant must check the box under Part III – Child Information at the top of page A-5 of the LDSS-4882 to indicate that information for additional children is being provided.  

If the LDSS-4882C is used, the applicant must indicate the number of LDSS-4882C forms attached to the LDSS-4882 by entering the page information at the top of page 1 of each LDSS-4882C.  

Supporting Documentation Also Is Required

In addition, documentation must be provided to verify information on the LDSS-4882 and to support the CSEU’s efforts to establish paternity and to establish, modify or enforce an order of support.

The LDSS-4882 and any available supporting documentation must be returned to the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit.
