Cooperation Requirements for TA Cases

TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). applicants/recipients must cooperate with the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit to locate the NCP Non-Custodial Parent. Sometimes called the "absent parent.", establish paternity, and establish, modify, and enforce orders of support.  

To satisfy this requirement, all TA applicants/recipients must complete the LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882) and provide any needed supporting documentation to the CSEU.  

If deemed necessary by the CSEU, the customer must appear at the CSEU for an interview. It also includes situations where rape or incest resulted in the conception of the child and situations where the mother is considering placing the child for adoption. or domestic violence, or a completed and signed LDSS-4281, Attestation to Lack of Information.  For additional information on the cooperation standard, refer to 99 ADM 05.
