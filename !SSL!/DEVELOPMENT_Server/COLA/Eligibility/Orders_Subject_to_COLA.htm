Orders Subject to COLA

Except as otherwise noted, the COLA Cost-of-Living Adjustment, sometimes called a Cost-of-Living Allowance. is applicable to all orders enforced pursuant to Title 6-A of the Social Services Law on behalf of persons in receipt of temporary assistance (TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA).), and which include family assistance and safety net assistance and which meet the eligibility criteria. (See “Eligibility” section of this document.)

The COLA is applicable to all other orders receiving services under Title 6-A, including orders for Title IV-E Foster Care and Medicaid-only cases. Orders enforced on behalf of persons not in receipt of public assistance (Non-TA Non-Temporary Assistance) are subject to COLA under Section 111-g of the Social Services Law, upon request of the CP Custodial Parent or Custodial Party. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. or NCP.

In Title IV-E Foster Care cases where there is an assignment of support rights, the SSD Social Service District shall be deemed to have requested the COLA review.
