CP and NCP decide: Object to COLA, or not?

Upon receiving an Adjusted Order of Support An order directing payments of child support, spousal (husband or wife) support, or both., the CP Custodial Parent or Custodial Party. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. and NCP Non-Custodial Parent. Sometimes called the "absent parent." have 35 days from the date of the mailing of the Adjusted Order of Support to file an objection A written request that a Judge review a decision of a Support Magistrate. to it.

Either party (even the one who requested the COLA Cost-of-Living Adjustment, sometimes called a Cost-of-Living Allowance.) can file an objection to a COLA.

An objection must be filed directly with the court.
