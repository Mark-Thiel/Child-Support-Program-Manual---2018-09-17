ASSETS runs its COLA monthly process (on the next third full weekend of the month)

On the next third full weekend of the month, ASSETS Automated State Support Enforcement and Tracking System (ASSETS). runs its COLA Cost-of-Living Adjustment, sometimes called a Cost-of-Living Allowance. monthly process.

ASSETS identifies the orders for which the CP Custodial Parent or Custodial Party. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. or NCP Non-Custodial Parent. Sometimes called the "absent parent." has requested a COLA (i.e., those cases which now have a Code 2 or Code 3 in the COLA Request Indicator field on the Obligation Tab screen).

For each of those orders, the CP, NCP, SCU Support Collection Unit., and the court will be sent an Adjusted Order of Support An order directing payments of child support, spousal (husband or wife) support, or both. this month.

For each of those orders, ASSETS generates data files and sends them to the Processing Center Centralized location where payment processing, notice generation, the Child Support Helpline (call center) and other important functions of the child support program are completed., along with all other files involved in the COLA monthly process.

ASSETS sends the data files to the Processing Center during the next batch A list of financial transactions to be entered or processed. Transactions may move money from one ledger to another within a single account or from one account to another account. The cover letter that goes out with the notice will bear the date the notice is generated by ASSETS -- not the date that it is printed at or mailed from the Processing Center.
