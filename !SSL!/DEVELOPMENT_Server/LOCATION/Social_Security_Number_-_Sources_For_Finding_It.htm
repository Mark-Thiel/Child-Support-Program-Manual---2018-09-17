Social Security Number:  Sources For Finding It

Source: 1989 Manual

The Social Security number of a PF Putative Father. This is a person who is presumed to be the father of a child, though there may not yet be hard evidence to support that presumption. is critical in the effort to locate and confirm the identity of that person and his/her resources.

To help find that information, check these resources:

 

Where You May Be Able to Find the SSN Social Security Number. for a PF:

1.

 Payroll stubs; Forms W-2 or 1099

2.

 Unemployment Insurance Benefits Booklet

3.

 Statement of Benefits for Workers' Compensation

4.

 Tax Returns; particularly any joint returns filed during the past seven years (in cases involving joint returns, PF/Respondent's SSN can be traced using the recipient's SSN)

5.

 Any of his/her correspondence with the Tax Department

6.

 Military documents (Note: The military currently uses the Social Security number as the serial number)

7.

 Life insurance policy

8.

 Credit applications

9.

 Automobile insurance policy

10.

 Credit cards

11.

 Credit agreement for purchases (such as furniture, refrigerator, television)

12.

 Old ID cards (health insurance, school ID, alien registration card)

13.

 Marriage license

14.

 Bank books (active or retired)

15.

 Any personal documents from the Social Security Administration

16.

 Documents from the Veterans Administration

17.

 College or school records

18.

 Medical or dental records or bills

19.

 Public assistance/WMS Welfare Management System. The WMS system used by the TA units supplies referrals and other information to the ASSETS system used by Child Support units.. The relationship is one-way only: WMS can update some information in the ASSETS system (CP residential addresses, for example), but the ASSETS system cannot update any datafields in the WMS system. records
