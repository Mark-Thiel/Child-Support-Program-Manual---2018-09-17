Local Resources for Location Investigation — a Checklist

Source: 1985 Manual

Review this list with the customer and see if she/he can provide the information needed.

If not, contact the specific sources directly.

  1.  Contact Post Office for last known address

  2.  Contact utility companies for disconnection or transfer of services

  3.  Contact last known employer and request information:

      1.  Employment status with employer

      2.  Date and reason for termination

      3.  Last home address in employer's file

      4.  Names and addresses of companies that have requested references

      5.  Name, relationships, and number of dependents declared on W-4 forms

      6.  Address that last W-2 form was mailed to

      7.  Respondent's Social Security number

      8.  Name and address of Respondent's insurance company

      9.  Name of bank in which Respondent's checks were deposited

      10.  Names and addresses of friends on or off the job

  1.  Contact people who know the Respondent The person or agency against whom a petition if filed, and who responds to the petition. (The respondent is known as the “defendant” in other types of courts.) (http://www.nycourts.gov/courts/7jd/courts/family/Definitions.pdf)

      1.  Parents

      2.  Siblings

      3.  Physicians

      4.  Last known landlord or neighbors

      5.  Minister, priest, rabbi, imam, etc.

      6.  Local community clubs or lodges

      7.  High school, college, university, etc.

  1.  Contact bank, loan company, credit bureaus, and other financial institutions, and request information on:

      1.  Checking and savings accounts

      2.  Loan agreements

      3.  Mortgages and/or property ownership

  1.  Contact District and/or Circuit Court and obtain information regarding:

      1.  Titles of Deeds

      2.  Voter registration address

      3.  Tax rolls

      4.  Divorce actions and/or orders

      5.  Civil lawsuits

      6.  Criminal actions

  1.  If a search turns up arrest records, contact:

      1.  Police and/or sheriff department for record of arrest

      2.  State Bureau of Identification for criminal record

      3.  Department of Corrections

      4.  Parole and/or Probation Officer

  2.  Review Family Court and Supreme Court files for:

      1.  Social Security number

      2.  Last known address

      3.  Employer leads

      4.  Other identifying information

  3.  Conduct statewide name/SSN Social Security Number. search of WMS Welfare Management System. The WMS system used by the TA units supplies referrals and other information to the ASSETS system used by Child Support units.. The relationship is one-way only: WMS can update some information in the ASSETS system (CP residential addresses, for example), but the ASSETS system cannot update any datafields in the WMS system. database
