Account Creation Summary

In direct-from-court cases, the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit worker should retrieve the ACS Account Creation Summary. The ACS is an ASSETS-generated Family Court report. It can provide info including the parties' and children's names, addresses, SSNs, DOBs, employment statuses, name of NCP's employer, custodian data, TA status, medical insurance, and support orders payable through the CSEU. The ACS can be retrieved via the UCMS (Universal Case Management System), or through ASSETS Summary mode, Court Information tab..  It contains a wealth of information useful in case-building. The Account Creation Summary can be accessed through the UCMS Universal Court Management System. This system pertains to Family Court only. Through the UCMS, workers can retrieve an ACS (Account Creation Summary) containing names, SSNs, DOBs, and much more..

What information can the Account Creation Summary provide?

The ACS, an ASSETS Automated State Support Enforcement and Tracking System (ASSETS).-generated [family] court report, can provide information regarding:

    • names of the parties

    • names of the children

    • addresses of the parties

    • SSNs of the parties

    • dates of birth of the parties and children

    • each party's employment status

    • name of the NCP's employer, if employed

In direct-from-court cases, the Account Creation Summary (ACS) Report should be retrieved or the UCMS Universal Court Management System. This system pertains to Family Court only. Through the UCMS, workers can retrieve an ACS (Account Creation Summary) containing names, SSNs, DOBs, and much more. inquiry function accessed in order to obtain the information for case-building.

(If the ACS is not available, information about the parties can be found by viewing the roster information on the Court Inquiry page of the UCMS inquiry function.)

For instructions on how to retrieve the ACS Family Court report or view roster information on the Court Inquiry page of the UCMS inquiry function on ASSETS, refer to the ASSETS User Guide.
