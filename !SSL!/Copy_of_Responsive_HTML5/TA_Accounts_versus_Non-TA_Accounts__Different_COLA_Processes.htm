TA Accounts versus Non-TA Accounts: Different COLA Processes

 

Eligibility criteria for a COLA on TA and Non-TA accounts are the same -- but processes are different.

 

  • TA Accounts: When account becomes eligible for a COLA, the DSS automatically requests one. Unless thereâ€™s an objection, the COLA will automatically go into effect.

 

  • Non-TA Accounts: CP or NCP must request a COLA.

 

In both types of cases, the ASSETS system automatically recognizes when an account is eligible for a COLA. TA and Non-TA accounts are processed on the same schedule, on the third full weekend of each month. Accounts are processed and COLA notices are mailed out in the month in which the order was issued or most recently modified or adjusted.

 

All COLA eligible accounts with January and February order dates are processed during the third full weekend of February. No orders are processed in the month of January each year, as the CPI-U average annual percentage change is not available until the very end of January.

 

COLA eligible accounts with March order dates are processed the third full weekend of March, and so forth throughout the rest of the year.
