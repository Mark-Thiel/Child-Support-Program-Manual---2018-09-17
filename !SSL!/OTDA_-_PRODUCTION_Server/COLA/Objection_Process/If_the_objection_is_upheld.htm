If the objection is upheld

If the objection to the COLA Cost-of-Living Adjustment, sometimes called a Cost-of-Living Allowance. is upheld, the Adjusted Order of Support issued to the CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. and NCP must be rescinded:

    • The worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. clears the Adj. Order Objection Date field.

    • The worker rescinds the Adjusted Order of Support by entering Code "R" (rescind) in the COLA Request Indicator field on the Cost of Living Adjustment (COLA) Information Edit screen.

The Court Order Date is the earlier of the date of the court determination on the Obligation Tab screen or the Adj Order Effective Date on the COLA Tab screen (i.e., 60 days from the date the adjusted order was mailed).
