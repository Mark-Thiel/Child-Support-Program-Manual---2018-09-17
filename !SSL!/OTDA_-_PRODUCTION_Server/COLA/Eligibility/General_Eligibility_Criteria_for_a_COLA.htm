General Eligibility Criteria for a COLA

The child support program can increase the amount of a child support order administratively (i.e. without court involvement) by applying a COLA Cost-of-Living Adjustment, sometimes called a Cost-of-Living Allowance. if:

1)    It has been at least two years since the support order was established, or since the obligation amount has been adjusted or modified,

AND

2)    the sum of the percentage changes in the CPI-U The Consumer Price Index for All Urban Consumers (CPI-U) is a measure of the average change over time in the prices paid by urban consumers for a market basket of consumer goods and services. The figure is released by the U.S. Bureau of Labor Statistics. for the years since 1994, or since the original support order, or since the last year in which the order amount was adjusted or modified — whichever is latest  — is equal to or greater than 10%.
