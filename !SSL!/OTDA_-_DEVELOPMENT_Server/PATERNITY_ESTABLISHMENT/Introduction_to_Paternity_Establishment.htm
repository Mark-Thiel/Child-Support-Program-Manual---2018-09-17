Introduction to Paternity Establishment

Source: 03 ADM 05 Notice Regarding Child Support Requirements for Victims of Domestic Violence.:

CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit workers conduct interviews with applicants/recipients and NCPs and gather information and evidence, including genetic test results, which support the allegation of paternity.

When paternity is contested, and a petition seeking an order of filiation has been filed in the local family court, a LDSS or County attorney will apear in court on behalf of the CSEU. The applicant/ROS Recipient of Services. This may be a CP receiving child support services, or it may be an NCP receiving paternity establishment services or other services. In some cases, both CP and NCP may be receiving services. may or may not be required to appear in court at th ttime of a hearing, depending on the facts and circumstances of a case.

If paternity is not contested, the procedure for obtaining acknowledgement of paternity can be done administratively (i.e. without court involvement). This option is made available to unwed parents in the hospital with the birth of the out-of-wedlock child.
