Define "Cooperation" with the Child Support Program

Source: 03 ADM 5

To determine whether or not a CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. applicant is cooperating with child support program, the worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. needs to evaluate the CP's willingness to provide information necessary for location of the NCP and paternity establishment.

Unwillingness to cooperate is different from -- and should not be confused with -- lack of information on the part of the CP, or fear of harm, violence, or retaliation from the NCP.

"Cooperation" means providing the following information about the customer, the child, and the NCP:

    • Name

    • SSN Social Security Number

    • DOB

    • Address

    • Employer's name and address (for NCP)

In addition, the client must:

    • Attend scheduled appointments with the child support program

    • Appear in court when necessary

    • Allow for genetic testing to establish paternity, when appropriate

    • Relinquish to the LDSS any payments that are received from the NCP (i.e. "assignment of rights")

    • Provide additional information as requested
