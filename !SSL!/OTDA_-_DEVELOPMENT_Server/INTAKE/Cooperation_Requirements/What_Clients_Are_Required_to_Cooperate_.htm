Who Is Required to Cooperate with the Child Support Program?

For details regarding cooperation requirements, see 99 ADM-05

Which clients are required to cooperate with the child support program -- and why?

The child support program was established to offset the cost of families receiving temporary assistance (aka public assistance) from the state.

Therefore, families receiving temporary assistance or receiving Medicaid benefits from New York State are required -- as a condition of receiving such benefits -- to cooperate with the child support program.

As outlined in 99 ADM-5, dated July 1,1999, a CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. who is applying for child support services must make a good faith effort to cooperate with the child support program, including providing information that can lead to identifying and locating a legally responsibility NCP for the purpose of collecting child support payments.

Medicaid clients must cooperate in obtaining medical support from noncustodial parents, to offset the taxpayers' costs for providing medical coverage.

Temporary Assistance for Needy Families (TANF)

Cooperation Requirement?  Yes.

TANF, or "TA" applicants or receipients. TA clients are required to cooperate with the child support program when there is an absent parent, or paternity for a dependent child has not legally been established.

MA-Only

Medicaid receipients

Cooperation Requirement?  Yes.

Child support services are available to families eligible for Medicaid when a parent is absent or when paternity needs to be established. These families are required to pursue medical support from the absent parent.

Title IV-E Foster Care cases Foster Care

Cooperation Requirement?  No.

Appropriate Title IV-E (Foster Care) cases are referred to the child support program, as determined by the foster care unit.

Non-IV-E Foster Care

Cooperation Requirement:?  No.

Certain foster care cases which are not eligible for Title IV-E Foster Care payments are referred to the child support program.

Non-TA

(Non-TANF)

Cooperation Requirement?  No.

In Non-TA (TANF) cases, the applicant might be a custodial parent, a caretaker relative, a legal guardian, or a representative of a private or public charitable institution.

Cooperation Requirement?  No.
