Non-Cooperation of a TA or IV-E or MA-Only Client

  1. If at any time an TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA)./IV-E Foster Care cases/MA-Only client demonstrates unwillingness to cooperate with the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit, advise the client that she/he is required to cooperate in order to be eligible for Public Assistance/Medical Assistance, and that eligibility may be jeopardized if there is no good cause for refusal.

  2. Explain to the client the circumstances that are acceptable as good cause for non-cooperation.  Those circumstances that are: [18NYCRR 369.2(b)]

      1. Cooperation in establishing paternity or securing support is reasonably anticipated to result in any of the following:

        • Physical harm to the child in the case

        • Emotional harm to the child in the case

        • Physical harm to the parent or caretaker relative with whom the child is living which reduces that person's ability to care for the child

        • Emotional harm to the parent or caretaker relative with whom the child is living, of such nature or degree that it reduces that person's ability to care for the child

      1. The child was conceived as the result of rape or incest

      2. Legal proceedings for the adoption of the child are pending before a court.

      3. The applicant or receipient (?) of the child is being counseled on whether to give the child up for adoption, and the counseling has not gone on for more than 3 months.

These are the only circumstances that are acceptable as good cause for non-cooperation.

If an MA-only applicant or recipient is pregnant, or if less than 60 days have passed since she gave birth, she does not have to cooperate with the CSEU as a condition of eligibility for medical assistance. {42 USC 1912] The MA Unit will not refer such cases to the CSEU unless the client herself wants child support services.  However, after sixty days have passed following the birth, the client must then cooperate with the CSEU if she wishes to continue receiving medical assistance, unless she has good cause for not cooperating.  If the client wishes to claim good cause, follow the procedures in Sub-section f. (1) through (3) above.
