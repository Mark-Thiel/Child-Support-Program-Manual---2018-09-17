Is a signed request for services attached to the order?

A signed request for services may be in the form of:

  • petition (signed prior to hearing)

  • addendum (signed at the hearing) — Form 4-3b, Addendum to Support Petition — Request for Child Support (IV-D) Services. (See 03 ADM 5 for details)

  • motion (supreme court)

If no signed request is attached to the order, the worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. must check the Court Interface, and look for a petition, addendum, or motion.

If one is found, the worker starts building the case.

If not found, the worker mails a 4882 to the CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child.. (Do not start building case until you have a valid, signed request for services.
