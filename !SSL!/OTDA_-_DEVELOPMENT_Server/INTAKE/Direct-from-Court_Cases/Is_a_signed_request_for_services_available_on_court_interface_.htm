Is a signed petition available on the court interface?

If so, were child support services requested in the petition?

(Petition A petition is a court document reflecting a request made prior to a hearing. A petition may be attached to a court order.:  A petition is signed before a hearing.

Addendum An addendum is a court document. It reflects changes to an order made on-the-fly during a hearing. A request for child support services may be contained in an addendum, a petition, or a motion, but must be signed by the requesting party.: Is made up on-the-fly in court, so it would be dated with the court date.)

See DCL dated March 7, 2016 -- Re:  Addendum to Family Court Support Petition to Request Child Support Services

 

Do the court order and the petition combined contain enough information to build the case?

If so, build the case (meaning, forward on to collection/enforcement.)

If not, worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. must mail a 4882 to the CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child..
