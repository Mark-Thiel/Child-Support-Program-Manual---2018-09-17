"Good Cause" versus Domestic Violence Waivers

Differences between "Good Cause" and "Domestic Violence"

Good cause [for non-cooperation with the child support program] requires documentation to prove the situation, whereas a waiver is granted by a Domestic Violence Liaison (DVL Domestic Violence Liaison.) after a comprehensive assessment interview with the client who answered "yes" to being a victim of domestic violence.

The domestic violence waiver does not require strict verification documentation, and is different from Good Cause.

Please do not confuse the two.

Good Cause

Good Cause is determined by the TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). Unit. The following are legitimate reasons for good cause:

    1. The CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. feels that cooperation with child support will result in serious physical and/or emotional harm to themselves or to the child.

    2. The child was conceived as a result of rape or incest.

    3. A legal adoption is pending or the parent is receiving pre-adoption services.

Referral Procedure for Domestic Violence Screening

Sometimes, a client may not make a disclosure of domestic violence when initially screened during a TA eligibility interview, but later discloses domestic violence during a child support interview, when compliance with the TA program requirements are discussed in more detail.

Child support workers cannot conduct a domestic violence screening.

Local district procedures and protocols for referring clients to be screened must be followed. (Some districts may refer the client back to the TA Unit, which will do the screening and make a referral to the DVL. Other districts may refer the client directly to the DVL.)

Therefore, it is important to know and follow the procedures in your own particular local district.
