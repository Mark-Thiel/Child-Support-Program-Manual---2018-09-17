Safety Concerns - Good Cause and Domestic Violence

Source:  03 ADM 5

I have been told that ANYONE can request the following actions (or suppression of sensitive information) and that they don't need a determination by TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). or DVL Domestic Violence Liaison.. Is that correct?

If a customer wants child support services but are afraid for his or her safety, the child support program can work with the customer in the following ways:

    • Remove the customer's location and/or employment information from petitions, notices, or any required financial disclosure, subject to approval of the court.

(Note: While a customer's address can be suppressed from all forms, it is not always possible to suppress the name of the county CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit or the court that issued the order.)

    • Request that the court not disclose location or employment information about the customer

    • Request that any court-ordered laboratory appointments (for genetic testing, for example) be scheduled for separate dates for the CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. and the NCP.

    • File paternity and support petitions on behalf of customer without requiring the customer to appear in court with the alleged abuser, or, if testimony of customer is required, request that the court make adequate provision for the customer's safety while in court

    • Provide all enforcement services without involvement of the customer
