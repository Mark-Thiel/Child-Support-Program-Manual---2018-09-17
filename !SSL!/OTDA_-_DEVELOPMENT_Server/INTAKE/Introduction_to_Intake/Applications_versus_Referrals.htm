Applications versus Referrals

Broadly speaking, the types of cases that workers build or manage can be put into one of two categories. They are:

    • Those in which the client is required to cooperate with the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit because they are receiving or applying to receive some form of temporary assistance from the state of New York ("Referrals"). For these cases, an intake interview may be necessary.

    • Those in which the client is not required to cooperate with the CSE Child Support Enforcement in order to meet eligibility requirements for other social services or temporary assistance, but rather is seeking child support services of his or her own volition. ("Applications") Note that this could include not only CPs, but, NCPs applying for services such as establishment of paternity. For these cases, an intake interview is not mandatory, but may be beneficial for purposes of location, paternity establishment, or case-building.

    In all cases, customers must cooperate with the CSEU in good faith level of cooperation etc etc or the case can be closed for lack of cooperation.

Applications: The client is not required to cooperate with the CSEU

You may sometimes hear applications referred to (by various workers, or in various policy directives) as:

    • Applications (as opposed to Referrals)

    • Private cases

    • Non-TA Non-Temporary Assistance cases

    • Non-PA Public Assistance. This is an old term for what is now called "Temporary Assistance." cases

    • Non-TANF Temporary Assistance to Needy Families. cases

    • Non-BA cases

    • Non-Public Assistance cases

    • Non-SNA Safety Net Assistance (SNA) falls under the umbrella of "Temporary Assistance). cases

Referrals: The Client is required to cooperate with the CSEU

These cases are commonly called "referrals" or "TA cases." (Note that MA cases also are sometimes referred to as "TA cases," since Medicaid is a form of public assistance, and intake procedures for the two are the same.)

Clients are required to cooperate with the CSEU if they are receiving or applying to receive temporary financial or medical assistance from the state.

You may sometimes hear referrals referred to (by various workers, or in various policy directives) as:

    • Referrals (as opposed to Applications)

    • TA Cases The term "TA cases" is used as an umbrella term to describe clients receiving any of the following types of temporary assistance: SNA; SNAP; MA; Family Assistance; and Safety Net.

    • DSS Department of Social Services cases

    • PA cases

    • TANF cases

    • BA cases

    • Public Assistance cases

    • SNA cases

    • MA cases -- (Note that MA cases also are sometimes referred to as "TA cases," since Medicaid is a form of public assistance, and intake procedures for the two are the same.)
