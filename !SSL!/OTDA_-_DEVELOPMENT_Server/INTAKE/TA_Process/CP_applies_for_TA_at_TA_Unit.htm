CP applies for temporary assistance at TA Unit

The CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child. applies for TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). at the TA Unit.

(This is done using form LDSS-2921, "New York State Application for Certain Benefits and Services.")

TA worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. asks customer whether there are any safety concerns.

If there are no safety concerns, the TA Worker gives the customer an LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882) (This form has replaced form DSS-2860.).

The TA worker, before handing the LDSS-4882 to the customer, should:

    1. Put a check mark in Box 2 in Section C of the LDSS-4882

    2. Fill out relevant parts of the "Agency Use Only" box at the bottom of Section C of the 4882 -- in particular, the "Date of Application" field.

If there are safety concerns, TA Unit does not give customer a 4882. Instead, they send customer to DVL Domestic Violence Liaison..

Once a DVL makes a determination about a full waiver, partial waiver, or no waiver, they (the TA Unit) need(s) to communicate the decision to the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit, because even though the case may never actually make it to the CSEU (because a waiver negates the cooperation requirement), the case may still show up on the Interface Report.

If this is a case of Good Cause, the TA Unit notifies the CSEU via LDSS-2859 Information Transmittal Form. (See Mary Hans first packet for photochopy of this form). This form is by child support, temporary assistance (TA), foster care (FC) and other programs to transmit case information. It is also used by Medicaid (MA) to receive child support information..

When a customer is referred to a DVL, there are several possible outcomes:

    • Full Waiver If a DVL grants a TA applicant/recipient a "full waiver" of the requirement for a TA applicant/recipient to cooperate with the child support program, all case and enforcement activity in child support must stop. A full waiver can be granted only by a Domestic Violence Liaison. The DVL will notify the CSEU of this status. (Compare to: "partial waiver.")

    • Partial Waiver If a DVL grants a TA applicant/recipient a "partial waiver" of the requirement to cooperate with the child support program, the CSEU may proceed with a child support case, but with certain precautions (such as suppressing the CP's address on petitions). A partial waiver can be granted only by a Domestic Violence Liaison. The DVL will notify the CSEU of this status. (Compare to: "partial waiver.")

    • No Waiver

    • Good Cause (rather than Domestic Violence)
