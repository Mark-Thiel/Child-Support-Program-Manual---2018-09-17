Worker interviews the customer

As part of the intake process, the CSEU Child Support Enforcement Unit may conduct an interview with an TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA)./IV-E Foster Care cases client before that client is actually approved to receive Temporary Assistance payments.  

This meeting is called a pre-acceptance interview.  It provides the opportunity to gather additional information concerning the client, absent parent/putative father, and dependent children. 

During the intake interview, the worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. should, ask the client to provide specific information needed to locate the absent parent/putative father and proceed with other actions (e.g. paternity establishment, support establishment, enforcement).  

Promptly enter into ASSETS Automated State Support Enforcement and Tracking System (ASSETS). all information gathered at a an intake interview.

Some districts fill out the application onscreen rather than on paper. These districts use electronic signature pads to capture the customer's signature. If this is done, the page containing the signature must be printed out and kept with the case files.

Note: All applications or referrals must include a SIGNED request for child support service from the customer. The signed document must be kept on file with the case record, and must be readily available in case of an audit.

Cases should not be built without a signed request (in the form of a signed LDSS-4882 "Information about Child Support Services and "Application/Referral for Child Support Services" intake form. (Form LDSS-4882) (This form has replaced form DSS-2860.), a signed petition from court, or other document.)

For paternity cases only:

Interview -- Possible Questions

How can this information be used (for location)?

What month did you become pregnant?

This information can be helpful in determining whether sexual relations between the individuals was occurring near or during the date of conception.

When did sexual relations begin/end?

This can help to determine if sexual relations between the individuals was occurring near or during the date of conception. This is a good follow up question to the previous question.

Did you have sexual relations with anyone other than this man the month before, during or after conception?

If so, someone else could be the biological father.

Names of other sexual partners during the period of conception? (Give 4882 NCP sheet to CP for completion)

What other putative fathers may be out there as well. Someone else may be the biological father.

Was your pregnancy full term?

So the worker can determine a close proximity of the date of conception of the child. If the last contact with the putative father was 9 months ago and the child was born premature the father being named probably isn’t the dad.

If your grant is denied or closes, do you still want Child Support services?

Determine  whether the district should build the child support case if the TA case is denied.
