Ineligible Orders or Accounts

The following types of orders are not eligible for a COLA:

  • $0 obligation amount orders.

  • Two-state orders in which New York does not have continuing exclusive jurisdiction (CEJ Continuing Exclusive Jurisdiction.).

  • Orders with a Status 02, 03, 04, or 05.

Also ineligible are accounts in which:

  • The NCP Non-Custodial Parent. Sometimes called the "absent parent." is active on TA Temporary Assistance. "Temporary Assistance" includes Family Assistance (FA) and Safety Net Assistance (SNA). at the time of case selection for the year's COLA Cost-of-Living Adjustment, sometimes called a Cost-of-Living Allowance. review.

  • Neither party has a valid New York State address in the ASSETS Automated State Support Enforcement and Tracking System (ASSETS). system.

  • The support order predates the CSSA Child Support Standards Act, enacted 15 September 1989. (9-15-1989) and the child is in receipt of TA and the Worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. determines that a COLA is not in the best interests of the child or the CP Custodial Parent. This is the parent with whom the child is living. However, the "Custodial Parent" could be a guardian such as a grandparent, foster home, or other person or entity with whom the child is living, and who is taking care of the child..

  • Orders issued by another state and registered in NYS only for enforcement.
