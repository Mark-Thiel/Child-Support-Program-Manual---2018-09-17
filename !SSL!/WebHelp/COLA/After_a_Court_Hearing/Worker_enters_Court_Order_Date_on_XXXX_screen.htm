Worker enters Court Order Date

THE FOLLOWING PERTAINS ONLY  TO INSTANCES IN WHICH THE OBJECTION WAS UPHELD.

The worker In the Child Support Services Operations Manual, the term "worker" (in upper- or lower-case) refers to personnel working in any child support function. enters the court order date on the ledger(s) affected by the order.

The Court Order Date is the earlier of the date of the court determination or the Adj Order Effective Date on Obligation Tab screen (i.e., 60 days from the date the adjusted order was mailed).

The court, upon hearing the objections, may issue an order which increases or decreases the order or is an order of no adjustment, based on the CSSA Child Support Standards Act, enacted 15 September 1989..
