Ability to Suppress Personally Identifying Information

This page is currently in section "Non-TA Cases (Intake) - Introduction." It should probably be moved to section "Safety Concerns."

The worker must advise the customer of:

    • The customer's right to request address confidentiality from Family Court.  

    • The CSEUâ€™s ability to suppress personally identifying information, such as the customer's residential address, on documents to be filed with the court and on other documents that pertain to the child support case.

If a customer wants services but is afraid for his or her safety, the CSEU can work with the him or her in the following ways:

    1. Remove the customer's location and/or employment information from petitions, notices, or any required financial disclosure, subject to approval of the court.

(Note: While a customer's address can be suppressed from all forms, it is not always possible to suppress the name of the county CSEU or the court that issued the order.)

    1. Request that the court not disclose location or employment information about the customer

    2. Request that any court-ordered laboratory appointments (for genetic testing, for example) be scheduled for separate dates for the CP and the NCP.

    3. File paternity and support petitions on behalf of customer without requiring the customer to appear in court with the alleged abuser, or, if testimony of customer is required, request that the court make adequate provision for the customer's safety while in court

    4. Provide all enforcement services without involvement of the customer

After receiving this information, and a discussion of the safety concerns, the customer wishes to apply for child support services, he or she must complete and sign the LDSS-4882.
