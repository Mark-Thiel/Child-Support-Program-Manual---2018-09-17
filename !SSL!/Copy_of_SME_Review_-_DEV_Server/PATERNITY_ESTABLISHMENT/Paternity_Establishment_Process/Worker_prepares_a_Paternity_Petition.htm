Worker prepares a Paternity Petition

Once the CSEU has obtained a verified address for a PF/NCP, it can prepare a Paternity Petition.

Source: Oswego Site Visit

  1. What is a Paternity Petition?

    A Paternity Petition is a formal, written application to a court requesting judicial action to determine legal fatherhood of a specific man for a specific child. The Petition for Paternity asks the Family Court to issue an Order of Filiation and a subsequent Order of Support for the child in the case.

      1. Paternity Petition - Person other than parent; Representative of Society  (Form 5-2)

      2. Paternity Petition - Social Services  (Form 5-3)

      3. Petition for Paternity Establishment (Parent)  (Form 5-1)

    A Paternity Petition must be verified by the petitioner. [FCA 523]  (See:  Who is the Customer? Who is the Petitioner?)

    Source: 1989 manual

    A Paternity Petition can be filed by [FCA 522]:

      • The mother of the child involved in the case

      • A man claiming to be the father of the child

      • A guardian, next of kin, or other person acting as a parent for the child

      • The child herself/himself

      • An authorized representative of an incorporated society doing charity or philanthropic work

      • A public welfare official where mother or child resides, if mother or child is likely to become a public charge

    The petition is generated through the Document Generation utility in ASSETS (Modules > Document Generation > NYS Family Court tab).

  2. What do you need to prepare a Paternity Petition?

    To prepare a Paternity Petition, you need a verified address for the PF/NCP.

  3. How do you prepare a Paternity Petition?

    1. In ASSETS, go to: Home > Modules > Document Generation > NYS Family Court tab

    1. Select the NYS Family Court tab.

    1. Select the Petitioner:

      • For TA cases, select Petitioner: Commissioner of Social Services, Assignee (DSS)

      • For Non-TA cases, select Petitioner: Child Support Services Applicant (CSS)

    1. Select Petition Type: Paternity Petition or Electronic Petition

    1. Select Filing Method: Paper Petition Filing.

    2. Select Include Documents: Personal Information Form (if required).

    1. If the customer has requested address confidentiality on the Personal Information Form, select Address Confidentiality Affidavit, as well.

    The Address Confidentiality Affidavit must be completed by the customer.

    1. Click the Continue button.

    The worker uses information provided on the LDSS-4882 and Affidavit Alleging Paternity to fill in fields on the petition.

    Datafields in the Personal Information Form will be auto-populated with information supplied by ASSETS.
