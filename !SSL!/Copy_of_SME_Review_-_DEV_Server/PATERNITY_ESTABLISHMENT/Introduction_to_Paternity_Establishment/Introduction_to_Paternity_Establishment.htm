Introduction to Paternity Establishment

Paternity establishment activities must be initiated as soon as possible based upon available information. Workers should check the Worker Alerts > Management Mode > Overage Statuses tab daily to see if any cases needing paternity establishment have been missed.

  1. Definitions of fatherhood

    1. "Putative father" generally means a man whose legal relationship to a child has not been established but who is alleged to be or claims that he may be the biological father of a child.

    2. "Biological father" is someone who has sired, rather than adopted, a child, and whose genes are therefore transmitted to the child.  A biological father is sometimes called a "birth parent" or "birth father."

    3. "Legal father" refers to a man recognized by law as the male parent of a child. A man is considered the legal father of a child if he was married to the child's biological mother at the time of the child's birth, or if paternity has been established through legal adoption, an Order of Filiation, or by signing an AOP.

  2. There are three ways in which legal paternity of a child can be established

      1. If the birth mother is married at any time during the conception, pregnancy, or the time of the birth of the child, her husband is legally the father.

      2. If both parents sign an AOP, this has the same force and effect as an Order of Filiation (i.e. it legally establishes fatherhood).

      1. If legal fatherhood is adjudicated and established in court, the court will issue an Order of Filiation. (This may be preceded by a genetic marker test.)

  3. How does the worker know whether paternity needs to be established?

    Source:  Fed Cert Doc

      1. The Worker Alerts > Management Mode > Overage Statuses tab reports a P Group as an open status for children needing paternity establishment.

      2. Workers should check the Putative Father Registry (PFR) to find out whether an AOP or an Order of Filiation has been filed or issued for an child. If not (and if the child was born out of wedlock), paternity needs to be established for that child.

      3. A Code 3, Code 4, Code 7, Code 9 in the Wedlock Indicator field on the Case Mode > Child tab indicates the need to establish paternity (and a P Group will automatically be set on the Case Mode > Status tab).

  4. Deadlines for Establishing Paternity

    • Paternity establishment proceedings can be initiated at any time before the child reaches 21 years of age. [FCA 517]

    • Paternity establishment proceedings can be initiated after the child reaches 21 years of age if the PF/NCP has previously acknowledged paternity in writing or furnished support. [FCA 517]
