    2. Create an account so that income withholding can be initiated and  payments can be credited to the account.

In order to build an account in ASSETS, you need an Order of Support from the court detailing the NCP's obligation amount.

The court order can be obtained directly from the court via mail or fax, or from an Account Creation Summary (ACS) in ASSETS.

Once the family court issues an Order of Support, the local district is responsible for ensuring that the order is carried out correctly. This process includes collecting, monitoring, and disbursing the money appropriately.

There Are Four Main Components to Support Collection

The four main components of support collection are:

    • Collection: receiving and sorting payments and deposits

    • Application: posting and verifying collections on accounts

    • Accounting: maintaining accurate ledgers and reports of monies received; making adjustments as necessary

    • Disbursement: paying the money to the correct party

Accessing and Translating Information From Court

The ACS is an electronic document that is created and stored in the ASSETS family court interface module. The Universal Court Management System (UCMS) interfaces with ASSETS via the family court interface and produces an ACS each time the court staff enter account information into UCMS.

 

When court staff enter the information, an ACS is generated to let you know that an account needs to be built or modified, and is available to you generally within 30 minutes of the court staff entering the information.

What Information Does the ACS Provide?

The ACS, an ASSETS-generated [family] court report, can provide information regarding:

    • Court order date; court action; type of obligation

    • Court information, including support magistrate and location

    • Obligation amount and frequency; how custodial parents will receive payments

    • Names of the parties/parents

    • Addresses of the parties

    • SSNs of the parties

    • Dates of birth of the parties and children

    • Each party's employment status and income

    • Name of the NCP's employer, if employed

    • Names of the children

    • Medical insurance

 

 

Translating information from the support order to the DSS-3117

Information obtained from the court order or the ACS must be interpreted and accurately translated into code that is then data entered into ASSETS to build an account. The DSS-3117 is used to translate the court information into system code, and accurately entering the data is crucial.

The DSS-3117 is a required State form used in local districts (SCUs) to manually record system codes before they are entered. Codes specific to the court order include the ones in the table below.

Other Information Needed to Create an Account

In addition to court-specific information, there are five key pieces of information necessary for building the account that must be translated into system code:

    • Wage Order Delinquency

(This is the amount past due that meets the criteria for initiating collection via the income withholding process.)

    • Billing Switch

    • Investigator Code

(This is assigned by your local district)

    • Standards Act Indicator

    • Zero Current Support Indicator

(Possible values are Y (Yes), N (No), and [ blank])
