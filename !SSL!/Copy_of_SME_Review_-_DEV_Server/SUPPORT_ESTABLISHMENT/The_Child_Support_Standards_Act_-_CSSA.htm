The Child Support Standards Act (CSSA)

On September 15, 1989 the Child Support Standards Act (CSSA) went into effect. This sweeping legislation (sometimes unofficially called the "guidelines") amended Section 240 of the Domestic Relation Law (DRL) and Section 413 of the Family Court Act (FCA), to require that the court determine child support obligation amounts by applying percentages to the combined parental income (CPI), ensuring that child support orders are consistent and uniform.

All child support ordesr are subject to the CSSA, and a chart can be used to determine the approximate basic annual child support obligation. The CSSA is based on two principles:

    • Both parents have a responsibility to contribute to the economic well-being of their children

    • Children should not bear the economic burden of parental separation
