Sequence of Events - Support Establishment

The CSEU should file a Support Petition as soon as possible after paternity has been established.

This is because:

    • Support can be retroactive to the filing date of the Support Petition

    • If the children are receiving temporary assistance (TA), support can be retroactive to the date of TA eligibility

  1. Financial Investigation

    Determine financial expenses of raising this particular child (e.g. special medical or educational needs?)

    To determine a noncustodial parent's ability to pay child support, child support staff will investigate the noncustodial parent's income and financial assets tthrough inquiries to employers and banks. They also conduct computer searches of the State Wage Reporting System (WRS), the New Hire Reporting System (New Hires), and state and federal income tax information.

    Two effective tools for obtaining financial information about the PF/NCP are:

      • New Hire Reporting (Financial information and information regarding the employer will auto-populate the case record.)

      • Wage and Health Benefits Report  (This can be presented to the court with the Support Petition.)

    Presenting complete and accurate financial information is essential for the court to determine a right-sized child support order. The income and resources, including medical insurance, available to each parent will be taken into account when determining the financial obligation of the PF/NCP.

  2. Financial documents required for verification

    As indicated in the summons, financial documentation about each parent is requested by the court. It is the parents' responsibility to return this information to the court before the hearing or the day of the hearing, whichever the court requests. The documentation includes:

      • A financial disclosure affidavit (included with summons)

      • A representative pay stub of each parent's income

      • A copy of their most recently filed tax returns

      • Proof of any other child support court orders

  3. Medical Support

    Another important element of the financial investigation is determining the availability of medical insurance. The court order for support must contain provisions regarding medical insurance that include:

      • Who is required to provide medical insurance for the child

      • Whether medical insurance is available and affordable

      • If medical insurance is not available or affordable, the customer may be directed to apply for public coverage (i.e. Medicaid, Child Health Plus)

      • Directions in the event that medical insurance becomes available

  4. Obtain court histories of the parties

    Obtain court histories of the parties from the Family Court Interface on ASSETS. Information available through the Family Court includes the following:

      • Orders already established for the parties

      • Whether or not a previous Support Petition has been filed and/or dismissed by the court

      • Whether or not a Paternity Petition has been filed and adjudicated by the court

    To access the Family Court Interface via ASSETS, go to Modules > Court > Messages

  5. File a Support Petition

    Note that some districts require the customer/CP to take the Support Petition to court. Others file the Support Petition (on behalf of customer, in Non-TA cases, or on behalf of the district in TA cases?)

    Once the completed Support Petition is filed with the court, the court will do the following:

      1. Set a date for the hearing on the court calendar

      2. Make arrangements for the PF/NCP to be properly served (summons and financial forms)

      3. Conduct a support hearing

      4. Issue a support order

  6. The Role of the Support Magistrate

    A support magistrate presides over the family court hearing and has jurisdiction over the following types of child support petitions:

      • Paternity petitions

      • Support petitions

      • Modification petitions

      • Violation petitions

  7. Family court video

    The NYS OTDA has developed a video for customers and PF/NCPs entitled "What You Need to Know About Child Support Hearings and Services." The video describes rules and expectations when attending family court.

  8. The support hearing

    Each party, as well as any legal representation, is given the opportunity to present their financial documentation and testimony to the support magistrate.

    At the hearing, the support magistrate will review the documentation and testimony provided and issue a child support order specifying the court's decision. A copy of  the Support Order is then mailed to each party by the court. The CSEU can also obtain a copy of the order by accessing the Family Court Interface, or by mail or fax.
