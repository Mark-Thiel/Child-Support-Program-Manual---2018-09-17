Worker Alerts > IVA Mode > CANs On System Without CINs

The CANs On System Without CINS report lists cases opened in Temporary Assistance and Medicaid that have a IV-D Indicator saying they need child support services, but more than 15 days have passed and a child support case has not yet been built in ASSETS.

Note: Cases must be built within 20 calendar days of receiving a signed referral, per SSL Title 18, Section 347.18.

________________________________________________________________

In ASSETS, go to: Home > Worker Alerts > IVA Mode > Overage CANs tab  

The “Overaged” column indicates how far overdue the building of the child support case is.

There are four possible overaged ranges. They indicate cases that are overaged:

    • 16 to 30 days

    • 31 to 60 days

    • 61 to 90 days

    • Over 90 days

If the case is not built or rejected by the child support unit, it will stay on this report indefinitely, even as additional cases are added. This causes the report to continue to get longer and longer.

You can see exactly how many overage cases your county has by checking the "Count Only" box, then clicking the Search button.
