Group Codes and Their Meanings

Note:  A case can have multiple Group Codes attached to it at any given time.

Status Codes appear on the Case Mode > Status Information tab.

Group

Meaning

A

Account has been built for this case.  (Which means that a support order has been established.)

B

Pre-acceptance

C

Title IV-E Case (Foster Care)

D

Pre-Determination

E

Enforcement

F

Financial Investigation is ongoing.  A WHBR has been sent to an employer, and ASSETS is awaiting results. When the WHBR is returned to the Processing Center and the employer information is verified, ASSETS closes the F Group.

G

Good Cause

H

Desk Review

I

Income Withholding

J

Review and Adjustment

K

Summons Service

L

Location efforts are ongoing.

Automated location searches via APRRS and PLS are automatically relaunched every 75 days, for as long as the L Group remains open.

Once ASSETS has a verified address for the Respondent, ASSETS closes the L Group.

M

Medical

N

Support Petition

P

Paternity Establishment efforts are ongoing.

ASSETS automatically opens a P Group on a case in which paternity is not established, or in which the Wedlock Indicator field is set to "N" (No).

Q

Federal Case Registry referral

R

Reciprocal

S

Support

T

Spousal

U

Suspend Aging

V

Verified Address

W

Paternity Petition

X

TA case  (This indicates an open TA case)

Y

Non-TA case  (This indicates an open Non-TA case) (Applicant does not have CAN A CAN number is a CASE NUMBER pertaining to a specific child support CASE. An individual person may have multiple CAN numbers. This is different from a CIN number, which identifies an individual person and can span serveral services under the umbrella of social services. (See glossary entry for CIN number.) or CIN numbers.) If the customer subsequently goes on temporary assistance, the CSEU worker enters CAN and CIN numbers for the children, and ASSETS opens an X Group, signifying that this is now a TA case.

Z

Non-IV-D Case (Non-child support)
