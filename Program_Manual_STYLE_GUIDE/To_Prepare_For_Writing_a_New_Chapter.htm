To Prepare For Writing a New Chapter

(Sources and preliminary steps)

 

  1. Review content regarding subject matter in CS 101 Manual

  2. Review content regarding subject matter in PDP onsite training materials

  3. Review major policy directives mentioned in materials above

  4. Review material available on ERS (Policy Directives, videos, training, etc.)

  5. Review the federal certification document regarding systems processes

  6. Gather screenshots related to subject matter

  7. Gather documents related to subject matter (petitions, affidavits, forms)

  8. Write down what is known (about the subject of the chapter)

  9. Compile questions about the unknown

  10. Go on site visits; interview workers re procedures, and observe them performing procedures

  11. Ask SMEs what (is the minimum content that) needs to be covered

The SMEs typically decide not to include a lot of information (that is contained in training materials, the federal certification document, or policy directives) on the grounds that the info is unimportant, irrelevant, or outside the scope of the chapter or the manual, or if procedures or protocols vary significantly among districts. For this reason, I try to limit time spent on producing unwanted or extraneous content.

  1. Agree with SMEs on areas of the subject matter that will not be addressed in the first release of the chapter.

  1. Create diagram of process (map out the process)

  2. Develop drilldown content for roadmap diagram

  3. Write a draft. Draft includes roadmap, drilldown content, and any other topics/material deemed crucial to the chapter.
