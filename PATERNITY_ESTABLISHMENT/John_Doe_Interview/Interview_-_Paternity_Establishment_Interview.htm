The Paternity Interview

When a case requires paternity establishment, the first action typically is to conduct an information-gathering meeting (i.e. an interview) with the customer.

The purpose of this is to obtain enough information to be able to petition the Family Court for an Order of Filiation.

Conducting an interview requires skill, tact, curiosity, and a non-judgemental attitude and demeanor. This is particularly true in regard to paternity interviews, which necessarily involve questions that may be perceived as very personal and/or intrusive.

Interviewing customers is more an art than a science.  The workers who do it are typically called "investigators" or "examiners."  

Gathering complete information from the customer during the paternity interview is more efficient than gathering a minimum of information and then having to contact the customer each time additional information is necessary.

In a paternity interview, having fewer people in the room is often better than having more people in the room. For example, if the investigator is interviewing a 17-year-old girl about pregnancy/paternity issues, and her grandmother is sitting right next to her at the interview, the girl may not feel free to be entirely forthcoming with relevant facts. Interviewing her just one-on-one will probably yield more, and better, information.

What the Customer Should Bring to the Paternity Interview

Source: 1989 Manual

When arranging the interview, tell the customer to bring pertinent items to the meeting in order to speed case processing.

These items include:

 

 

What should the customer bring to a paternity interview?

1.

The child's birth certificate

2.

Medical records and bills for the pregnancy and delivery of the child

3.

A photo of the putative father

4.

Letters from the PF that discuss the pregnancy or child

5.

PF's Social Security number

6.

PF's address

7.

PF's employer's name and address

The CSEU should have a standard list of such items that they can give to the customer.

See also: Social Security Number - Sources for Finding It.
