The Acknowledgement of Paternity Form

Prior to signing an AOP, the Putative Father must be advised — verbally and in writing — of his rights and of the consequences of signing such an acknowledgement.

  1. What is an "Acknowledgement of Paternity" (AOP) form?

      • An Acknowledgement of Paternity (AOP) form is a legal document signed by unmarried parents acknowledging that they are the parents of a child.

      • An AOP has the same force and effect as an Order of Filiation (??MT - need link/copy). ??MT - Does that mean that they need one or the other, but not both?

      • Prior to signing an AOP, the Putative Father must be advised — verbally and in writing — of his rights and of the consequences of signing such an acknowledgement.

    Oral notice can be provided through an audio or video presentation of the "Acknowledgement of Paternity Form" video located at:  http://www.trainingspace.org/materials/child_support_videos/paternity.wvx.  

    The AOP form itself satisfies the written notice requirement.

      • If either parent has any doubts regarding paternity, the AOP should not be signed, and paternity should be established through a court hearing.

      • The AOP form must be completed and signed and witnessed by two people unrelated to the father or the mother.

      • The AOP form is available in a variety of languages.  However, only the completed English-language form is admissible.

      • Either parent has 60 days to rescind the AOP from the date of signing, or 60 days from the minor parent's 18th birthday.

     

  2. When to Use the AOP form

    If the AOP is not signed at the hospital, the worker may need to assist the parent in signing the form in the CSEU.  It is important for the worker to know when it's appropriate and when it is not appropriate to suggest that the parents sign the AOP.

    When to use the AOP form

      • The child is born out of wedlock

    OR

      • The mother is not married at any time during the pregnancy.

    When to NOT use the AOP form

      • If the mother is married at any time during the pregnancy

    OR

      • If either parent has any doubts about paternity

    See also:  Recanting or Rescinding an Acknowledgement of Paternity

  3. How to obtain an AOP form

    The AOP form is available in hard copy in your district office.  Your supervisor can order more, if needed, from the NYS Office of Temporary and Disability Assistance.  The form can also be found online at:  https://www.childsupport.ny.gov/dcse/pdfs/4418.pdf.

  4. Important requirements when completing an AOP form

      • The form must be completed with black ink.

      • Do not use correction fluid.

      • Do not cross out mistakes on the child's name or other information.

      • Do not leave blank boxes or missing information.

      • If any of the above errors occur, the form will be rejected.  If you make a mistake while filling out the AOP form, you must start with a brand-new form.

    AOPs, once signed, must be submitted to the court for approval.

  5. Rights of the PF — and the Consequences of Signing an AOP

    The rights of the PF and consequences of signing an AOP (as listed in SSL 11-k) are as follows:

      • The Respondent has the right to consult an attorney before making such an acknowledgement.

      • By executing an acknowledgement on the issue of paternity, the Respondent waives his right to a hearing to which he would otherwise be entitled.

      • The Respondent's Acknowledgement of Paternity will be filed with the Putative Father Registry and may establish the child's right to inheritance from the Respondent.

      • If the PF does not make such an acknowledgement, he can be held liable for support only if the Family Court, after a hearing, makes an order declaring that the Respondent is the father of the child and issues an order of support.

    The written notification to the PF of his rights and the consequences of signing an AOP must include language similar to that contained in SSL 111-k.

    If the Respondent is not advised of the above rights and consequences, a judge can dismiss the case. [SSL 111-k]

  6. Can an AOP be filled out at the CSEU offices?

    An AOP can be filled out at a CSEU only if the child was born in New York State.

  7. Where does the AOP form go once it's signed?

    The AOP form must be filed with the registrar for the hospital where the child was born.

    For example:

      1. Emily was born in Albany Medical Center hospital.

      2. Her parents did not sign the AOP in the hospital, but later they come to the CSEU and want to sign the form.

      3. The CSEU must file the AOP with the registrar in Albany County.

      4. The Albany County registrar will then file the form with the Department of Health, Vital Records Division, and the Putative Father Registry (PFR).  

  8. Recanting an Acknowledgement of Paternity

    When someone signs an Acknowledgement of Paternity form, they have sixty (60) days to recant.

    The following are possible legitimate reasons why recanting might be approved:

      • The AOP was signed under duress.

      • The AOP was signed fraudulently, or was fraudulently produced.

      • There is a material mistake of fact on the form.

  9. When a court vacates an Acknowledgement of Paternity

    When a court vacates an AOP, it sends a copy of the order vacating the AOP to the Department of Health (DOH).

    The DOH sends the information to the office of the Registrar, which officially seals the AOP and mails the customer a new birth certificate without the name of the person who had mistakenly, fraudulently, or unwillingly signed the AOP form.
